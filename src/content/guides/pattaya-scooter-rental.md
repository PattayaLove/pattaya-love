Renting a scooter is one of the best ways to explore Pattaya on your own terms. It gives you the freedom to move between beaches, restaurants, and neighborhoods without relying on baht buses or negotiating with taxi drivers. But there are real risks and a few things you need to know before you twist the throttle.

## Why Rent a Scooter

Pattaya is spread out. [Walking Street](/areas/walking-street/) to [Jomtien Beach](/areas/jomtien/) is about 5 kilometers. Central Pattaya to [Naklua](/areas/naklua/) is another 4 kilometers north. Public transport is limited to baht buses running fixed routes along Beach Road and Second Road, and Grab rides add up fast if you are moving around several times a day.

A scooter costs as little as 200 THB per day and lets you get anywhere in the city within 10 to 15 minutes. It is especially useful for reaching spots outside the main tourist strip, like [Pratumnak Hill](/areas/pratumnak/), [East Pattaya](/areas/east-pattaya/) restaurants, or day trips to nearby attractions.

## Popular Models and Prices

Rental shops in Pattaya stock a range of scooters and motorbikes. Here are the most common options.

### Honda Click 125

The workhorse of Pattaya rentals. It is light, easy to handle, and has enough power for city riding. Automatic transmission means no gears to worry about. This is the best choice for most visitors, especially if you are not an experienced rider.

**Price:** 200 to 300 THB per day, 2,500 to 4,000 THB per month.

### Honda PCX 150

A step up from the Click with more power, better suspension, and a more comfortable seat for longer rides. The PCX handles highway speeds better if you plan to ride to nearby towns like Na Jomtien or Bang Saray.

**Price:** 300 to 500 THB per day, 4,000 to 6,000 THB per month.

### Honda Forza 350

The premium option for experienced riders who want a larger, more powerful bike. The Forza is comfortable for long rides and has enough storage under the seat for a full-size helmet. It is overkill for short city trips but excellent if you want to explore further afield.

**Price:** 500 to 800 THB per day, 7,000 to 10,000 THB per month.

### Older Bikes and Budget Options

Some shops rent older Honda Wave 110 semi-automatics or beat-up Scoopy models for as low as 150 THB per day. These are fine for short trips around town, but inspect them carefully before renting. Check the brakes, tires, and lights.

## Where to Rent

Rental shops are everywhere in Pattaya. You will find them on Second Road, Beach Road, [Soi Buakhao](/areas/soi-buakhao/), and scattered throughout Jomtien. There is no shortage of options.

Established shops with a physical storefront and a fleet of well-maintained bikes are always preferable to someone renting a single bike from their apartment. Look for shops that have been around for a while, display clear pricing, and do not pressure you into taking a specific bike.

Some of the more reputable rental operations are found along Second Road between Soi 1 and Soi 10, as well as in the Jomtien area near Dongtan Beach.

## License Requirements

Technically, you need a valid motorbike license from your home country plus an International Driving Permit (IDP) to legally ride in Thailand. The IDP must specifically include a motorcycle endorsement.

In practice, police in Pattaya run occasional checkpoints, especially on Sukhumvit Road and the main roads connecting to Jomtien. If you are stopped without a proper license, the fine is typically 500 THB. It is a minor fine, but the bigger issue is insurance.

**Without a valid license, your travel insurance almost certainly will not cover you in an accident.** This is the single most important thing to understand about riding in Pattaya. Hospital bills from a serious motorcycle accident can run into hundreds of thousands of baht. Get an IDP before you travel. They cost around 15 to 20 USD in most countries and take minutes to obtain.

## Insurance

Most rental shops do not provide insurance. Your rental agreement, if one even exists, typically states that you are responsible for any damage to the bike.

Your options for coverage are:

- **Travel insurance from home** that covers motorbike use. Check the fine print, as many policies exclude motorcycles over 125cc or require a valid license.
- **Thai voluntary motor insurance** is available but rarely offered by rental shops.
- **Credit card travel insurance** sometimes covers rental vehicles but almost never covers motorbikes in Southeast Asia.

The safest approach is to carry proper travel insurance with motorbike coverage and a valid IDP before you ride.

## Helmet Law

Helmets are mandatory in Thailand. The fine for riding without a helmet is 500 THB, and police checkpoints specifically look for this. More importantly, head injuries are the leading cause of death in Thai motorcycle accidents. Always wear a helmet, even for short trips.

Most rental shops provide a helmet with the bike, but the quality varies. If the helmet feels flimsy or does not fit properly, consider buying your own at a Big C or Tesco Lotus for 300 to 600 THB. It is a small investment for serious protection.

## Fuel Costs

Filling up a Honda Click 125 from empty costs about 80 to 100 THB. A full tank gets you roughly 200 to 250 kilometers of riding, which is easily a week of city use for most visitors.

Petrol stations are common along Sukhumvit Road and the main roads. In more central areas, you will also see roadside vendors selling gasoline in glass bottles for around 40 THB per liter. These are convenient but slightly more expensive than station prices.

## Parking

Parking a scooter in Pattaya is generally easy. Most businesses, restaurants, and malls have free motorbike parking areas. Central Festival, Terminal 21, and other malls have dedicated covered motorbike parking.

On the street, park where you see other bikes parked. Avoid blocking sidewalks or parking in marked no-parking zones. Fines are rare but towing does happen in some areas, especially near Walking Street.

## Safety Tips

Pattaya traffic is chaotic by Western standards. Here are the essential rules for staying safe.

- **Ride defensively.** Assume every other driver will do something unexpected, because they will. Cars pull out without looking, trucks stop in the middle of the road, and other scooters weave in all directions.
- **Avoid riding at night if possible.** Visibility is poor, drunk drivers are common after 22:00, and road hazards like potholes and loose gravel are harder to spot.
- **Stay left.** Thailand drives on the left side of the road. It takes a few minutes to adjust if you are used to right-hand traffic.
- **Watch for sand and water on the road.** Especially around corners and near construction sites. These cause the majority of low-speed crashes.
- **Do not drink and ride.** This sounds obvious, but it is the leading cause of serious tourist injuries in Pattaya.
- **Lock the steering column** and use a disc lock if you have one, especially overnight. Bike theft is not rampant but it does happen.

## Common Scams to Avoid

**Passport as deposit.** Never hand over your passport. A reputable shop will accept a photocopy or a cash deposit of 2,000 to 5,000 THB. If they insist on your passport, walk away and find another shop.

**Pre-existing damage claims.** Before you take the bike, photograph every scratch, dent, and mark from multiple angles. Send the photos to yourself so they are timestamped. When you return the bike, some shops will claim damage you did not cause.

**Overcharging for scratches.** Minor cosmetic scratches should cost a few hundred baht to fix at most. If a shop demands thousands of baht for a small scratch, negotiate firmly or contact the tourist police at 1155.

**Fuel scam.** Some shops hand you a bike with a nearly empty tank and expect it back full, effectively charging you for a full tank. Check the fuel level when you pick up and note it in any paperwork.
