Pattaya's skyline has transformed over the past decade, and with it came a wave of rooftop venues that rival anything Bangkok has to offer — at a fraction of the price. Whether you want a sundowner with ocean views or a full fine-dining experience above the city lights, this guide covers the best elevated options.

## Top Rooftop Bars

### Horizon Rooftop — Hilton Pattaya

The undisputed king of Pattaya rooftops. Perched on the 34th floor of the Hilton, directly above Central Festival mall, Horizon offers 360-degree views of the bay, the city, and Koh Larn in the distance.

- **Vibe:** Sophisticated, upscale, perfect for dates
- **Drinks:** Cocktails 350-500 THB, wine from 300 THB/glass
- **Best time:** Arrive by 17:30 for sunset. The golden hour views here are unmatched
- **Tip:** No strict dress code, but smart casual is expected. Avoid flip-flops

### The Roof Sky Bar — Siam@Siam

Located in North Pattaya, this rooftop bar sits on top of the design-forward Siam@Siam hotel. The infinity-edge pool visible from the bar creates a dramatic visual, especially when lit up at night.

- **Vibe:** Trendy, Instagram-worthy, younger crowd
- **Drinks:** Cocktails 280-400 THB, craft beer available
- **Best time:** Weekend evenings for the best atmosphere
- **Tip:** Popular with Bangkok visitors — can get busy on long weekends

### Edge — Hilton Pattaya

Different from Horizon, Edge is the Hilton's outdoor restaurant and bar on the same level. More focused on dining with views rather than pure drinking.

- **Vibe:** Relaxed fine dining, couples and small groups
- **Food:** International menu, 400-1,200 THB per main course
- **Best time:** Dinner service from 18:00

### Mantra Restaurant & Bar

A multi-level venue in Central Pattaya that combines a ground-floor restaurant with a rooftop cocktail lounge. The rooftop space is smaller and more intimate than the mega-rooftops.

- **Vibe:** Sophisticated, food-focused
- **Drinks:** Cocktails from 300 THB
- **Specialty:** Their tasting menus are worth the splurge

## Rooftop Dining Tips

**Reservations:** For Horizon and Edge, book ahead on weekends and holidays — walk-ins are possible on weekdays but not guaranteed a prime table.

**Sunset timing:** In Pattaya, sunset ranges from about 17:45 (December) to 18:45 (June). Arrive 30-45 minutes before for the best light.

**Dress code:** Most rooftop venues in Pattaya are relaxed compared to Bangkok, but "smart casual" is the safe bet. Shorts are usually fine if they're neat; tank tops and flip-flops may get you turned away at the nicer spots.

**Budget:** Expect to spend 1,500-3,000 THB per person for drinks and a meal at the premium rooftops. That's still roughly half what you'd pay for a comparable experience in Bangkok.

**Rain plan:** November to early December is the tail end of rainy season. Most rooftop venues have covered sections, but check weather forecasts if you're planning a special evening.

## Beyond the Rooftops

If heights aren't your thing but views are, several beachfront restaurants along Jomtien and Pratumnak offer equally stunning sunset views at ground level. The restaurants along the Pratumnak hillside are particularly underrated — ocean panoramas without the premium pricing of hotel rooftops.

## Final Thoughts

Pattaya's rooftop scene is one of its most pleasant surprises for first-time visitors. The combination of ocean views, tropical sunsets, and prices that are genuinely affordable by international standards makes it an essential part of any Pattaya trip. Start with Horizon at the Hilton for the definitive experience, then explore from there.
