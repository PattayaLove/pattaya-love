## Overview

Pratumnak Hill sits on the elevated headland between Pattaya Beach and Jomtien Beach. It's Pattaya's quietest and most upscale residential area — a neighborhood of luxury villas, boutique hotels, and ocean-view condos that feels completely disconnected from the neon chaos below.

The area is popular with long-term expats and couples who want easy access to everything but prefer to sleep somewhere peaceful.

## The Vibe

Pratumnak is residential and relaxed. Tree-lined streets, ocean viewpoints, and a mix of Thai and international restaurants create a Mediterranean-village atmosphere. It's hilly — hence the name — which means many properties have stunning panoramic views of the Gulf of Thailand.

## Key Landmarks

**Big Buddha Hill** (Wat Phra Yai) sits at the northern end of Pratumnak and is one of Pattaya's most iconic landmarks. The 18-meter golden Buddha statue offers sweeping views across the city and bay. Free entry, modest dress required.

**Pattaya Viewpoint** at the southern tip of the hill gives you the classic Pattaya panorama — the curved bay, the skyline, and Koh Larn in the distance. Best at sunset.

## Where to Stay

The Royal Cliff resort complex dominates the area — four connected properties ranging from the family-friendly Beach Hotel to the ultra-luxury Royal Wing. Boutique options like the Cross Pattaya and various Airbnb pool villas offer alternatives.

## Dining

Pratumnak punches above its weight for dining. The hilltop has several excellent restaurants with sea views, particularly Italian and Mediterranean options. The Edge restaurant at the Hilton (technically next door) is a popular date-night destination.

## Tomorrowland Thailand 2026

Pratumnak's strategic position between Pattaya and Jomtien makes it an excellent base for Tomorrowland Thailand 2026 in November. Close enough to the expected festival grounds in the Jomtien/Na Jomtien area, but quieter than staying in the middle of the action.

## Getting Around

Pratumnak is best navigated by motorbike or Grab. The hills make walking less practical, and baht buses don't cover the area well. A scooter rental (200-300 THB/day) gives you the most freedom.
