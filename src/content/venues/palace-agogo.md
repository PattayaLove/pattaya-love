Palace occupies a prime spot in the middle section of Walking Street, where the foot traffic is heaviest and the competition for your attention is fiercest. In a stretch of road packed with GoGo bars, Palace holds its own by focusing on what matters: good entertainment at reasonable prices with staff who know how to make visitors feel welcome.

## What to Expect

The layout is straightforward and effective. A central stage dominates the room with seating arranged in tiers that give most positions a clear view of the shows. The interior is dark and atmospheric without being dingy, and the venue is kept clean and reasonably maintained.

Entertainment at Palace hits a consistent standard. Shows run throughout the evening with a mix of solo and group performances. The dancers are engaging and the energy builds as the night progresses, peaking between 22:00 and midnight. It is not the most extravagant production on Walking Street, but it is reliably entertaining and that counts for a lot on a street where quality can be unpredictable.

The mid-street location means Palace benefits from the natural flow of visitors moving up and down Walking Street. The bar often picks up spillover from busier neighbors, and on good nights the atmosphere can match any venue on the strip.

## Drink Prices

Beer is priced around 150 to 170 THB, making it one of the more competitively priced GoGo bars in the area. Cocktails range from 200 to 280 THB. Lady drinks come in at approximately 150 to 200 THB. The overall value proposition is strong, and Palace is a good option if you want the GoGo bar experience without stretching your budget.

No cover charge. Walk in and take a seat.

## Tips

Palace is a solid mid-range choice for a Walking Street GoGo bar. It does not try to be the best or the biggest, and that honesty in its approach is part of its appeal. The experience is straightforward, enjoyable, and fairly priced.

If the more famous GoGo bars have long waits or packed seating, Palace is an excellent alternative that rarely disappoints. Its central location makes it easy to pop in, check the vibe, and either stay or move on.

Arrive around 21:00 to 22:00 for the best seat selection. Smaller bills are useful for drinks and tipping, and the standard 20 to 100 THB tip range for dancers applies here as at most Walking Street venues.

Walking Street establishments can change with little notice. Palace has maintained a steady presence, but confirming operating status remains a smart practice, particularly during the quieter months when some bars adjust their schedules.
