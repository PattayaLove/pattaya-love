Lay Beach Club occupies a stretch of Na Jomtien coastline that feels worlds away from the neon-lit chaos of central Pattaya. Set slightly south of the main Jomtien tourist area, Lay offers a boutique beach club experience that prioritizes design, atmosphere, and a sense of escape. If Alexa Beach Club is Pattaya's mainstream beach club answer, Lay is its more refined, intentionally curated counterpart.

## The Setting

The first thing that strikes you is the architecture. Lay is built into a terraced oceanfront layout, with the infinity pool positioned as the centerpiece overlooking the Gulf of Thailand. The design borrows from Bali and Mediterranean influences — natural stone, flowing white curtains, low wooden daybeds, and carefully placed tropical plants that frame every angle for maximum visual impact.

The pool itself is the main draw. It runs along the edge of the property with an infinity edge that blends into the ocean horizon beyond. Surrounding the pool are tiered lounging areas with oversized daybeds, cushioned seating nooks, and private cabanas that can be reserved for groups. Every seat has a sea view, which is a simple promise that Lay actually delivers on.

## Music and Atmosphere

Lay keeps the music intentional and mood-driven. Afternoon sessions lean into balearic, lo-fi house, and chill electronic — the kind of soundtrack that enhances conversation rather than competing with it. As the sun drops, the tempo builds gently. Sunset is the main event, and the resident DJs know how to score it perfectly.

On select weekends, Lay hosts curated events with guest DJs and themed sessions that attract a crowd from Bangkok and beyond. These events are announced on social media and tend to sell out for premium seating, so checking ahead is advisable if you are planning around a specific date.

The overall atmosphere is calm, intentional, and quietly upscale. This is not a party-hard venue. It is a place to slow down, appreciate the setting, and enjoy quality drinks while the sun paints the sky.

## Drinks and Dining

The cocktail program at Lay is a step above standard beach club fare. Mixologists work with fresh, locally sourced ingredients — think butterfly pea flower gin and tonics, tamarind margaritas, and coconut-washed rum old fashioneds. Signature cocktails run 320 to 480 THB. Wine is available by the glass and bottle, with a curated list that leans European. Beers start at 190 THB.

Food follows the same philosophy: quality over quantity. The menu is compact but well-executed, featuring seafood-forward dishes, poke bowls, ceviche, grilled catch of the day, and lighter bites like bruschetta and hummus platters. Most dishes fall between 280 and 600 THB. The kitchen caters well to couples and small groups who want to graze through the afternoon.

## When to Visit

Lay opens daily from around 11:00 to 22:00. The ideal arrival window is between 14:00 and 15:00, which gives you a full afternoon of pool time before the sunset golden hour. Unlike some beach clubs where sunset is a brief moment, Lay's west-facing orientation means the light show stretches out for nearly an hour, making it the undisputed highlight of any visit.

Weekdays offer a quieter, more intimate experience — often you will have large sections of the pool area to yourself. Weekends bring a livelier crowd, particularly on Saturdays. For the best experience on busy days, book a daybed or cabana in advance through their website or social media.

## Who It Is For

Lay attracts couples, small groups of friends, and solo visitors who appreciate design-forward spaces and a slower pace. The crowd tends to be slightly older and more well-traveled than typical Pattaya venues — think Bangkok professionals on weekend escapes, visiting couples, and expats celebrating milestones.

## Getting There

Na Jomtien is roughly 20 to 25 minutes south of central Pattaya by car. A Grab ride from the city center costs 150 to 250 THB depending on traffic. The venue is well-signed along the Na Jomtien coastal road. Parking is available on-site for those with their own transport. If you are staying in Jomtien, it is a short 10-minute drive further south.
